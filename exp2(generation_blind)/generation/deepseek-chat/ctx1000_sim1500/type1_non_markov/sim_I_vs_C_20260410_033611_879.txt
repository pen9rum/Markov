Looking at the 1000-round history:

- **P2** plays **Paper** in **every single round**. That is 100% Paper, 0% Rock, 0% Scissors.  
  This is a **pure distribution strategy** — specifically **C (Pure Paper)**.

- **P1** plays Rock, Paper, Scissors in varying proportions.  
  Let’s check if P1 is reacting to P2’s previous move:  
  Since P2 always plays Paper, if P1 were Markov:
  - **Win-Last (X)**: Would play Scissors every round after the first (to beat Paper).  
    But P1 does not play only Scissors — so not X.
  - **Lose-Last (Y)**: Would play Rock every round after the first (to lose to Paper).  
    But P1 does not play only Rock — so not Y.
  - **Copy-Last (Z)**: Would play Paper every round after the first (to copy Paper).  
    But P1 does not play only Paper — so not Z.

  Therefore, **P1 is not Markov**.

**2. Identify distribution strategies**

- **P2** is **C (Pure Paper)** — confirmed.

  - Rock: 215 times (21.5%)
