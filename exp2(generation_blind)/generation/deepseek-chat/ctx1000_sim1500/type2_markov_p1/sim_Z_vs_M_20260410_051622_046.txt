**Step 1 — Strategy Inference**

P1 plays Paper ~70% of the time, Rock ~16%, Scissors ~13%.  
This matches **Paper-biased** distribution strategies:  
Strategy **I** (25% Rock, 50% Paper, 25% Scissors) is close but P1’s Paper rate is even higher.  
Strategy **M** (33.3% Rock, 50% Paper, 16.7% Scissors) or **N** (16.7% Rock, 50% Paper, 33.3% Scissors) don’t match because P1’s Scissors is lower than Rock.  
Actually, P1’s distribution is closest to **Paper > Rock > Scissors** with Paper ~70%, Rock ~16%, Scissors ~14%.  
None of the listed distributions have 70% Paper, but **I** is the closest among them (50% Paper). However, the observed 70% Paper suggests a stronger bias than any listed. But given the options, **I** is the best match among A–P.

**P2 moves:**

**Check for Markov patterns:**

Test if P1 reacts to P2’s previous move:  
Look at transitions:  
Example: Round 1 P2=Paper, Round 2 P1=Paper (not the move that would beat Paper, which is Scissors). So not Win-Last.  
Check Copy-Last: Round 1 P2=Paper, Round 2 P1=Paper (copy). But Round 2 P2=Rock, Round 3 P1=Rock (copy). Could be Copy-Last? Let’s check more systematically.

Take a sample:  
Round 9: P2=Scissors, Round 10 P1=Scissors (copy).  
Round 10 P2=Paper, Round 11 P1=Paper (copy).  
Round 11 P2=Paper, Round 12 P1=Paper (copy).

Round 14 P2=Paper, Round 15 P1=Paper (copy).  
Round 15 P2=Rock, Round 16 P1=Rock (copy).  
This is a perfect Copy-Last pattern for P1.

Check if it holds for all rounds after Round 1:  
Round 1 P2=Paper, Round 2 P1=Paper (copy yes).  
Round 2 P2=Rock, Round 3 P1=Rock (copy yes).  
Round 3 P2=Paper, Round 4 P1=Paper (copy yes).  
Round 4 P2=Paper, Round 5 P1=Paper (copy yes).  
…  
Yes, from Round 2 onward, P1 always copies P2’s previous move.  
So P1 is **Copy-Last (Z)**.

That means P2 is a distribution player (since at most one Markov player).  
P2’s distribution from history: Rock 29.2%, Paper 55.5%, Scissors 15.3%.

Given P2’s Scissors is only 15%, **M** fits best.

So:  
P1 = Z (Copy-Last)  
P2 = M (Paper > Rock > Scissors: 33.3% Rock, 50% Paper, 16.7% Scissors)
